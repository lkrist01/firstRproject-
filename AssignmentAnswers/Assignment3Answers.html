4 Generate a density plot

Use a combination of the functions ggplot() and geom_density() to create a density plot of the tail lengths of the Hawks within the hawksSmall data frame. Your result should something like this:

ggplot(data=hawksSmall,aes(x=Tail))+geom_density()+xlab("Tail  (mm)")+ylab("Density")

Recreate your plot with the argument adjust = 0.5 and adjust = 1. Describe the role played by the adjust argument within the geom_density() function.

ggplot(data=hawksSmall,aes(x=Tail))+geom_density(adjust=1)+xlab("Tail  (mm)")+ylab("Density")

How many modes does the data distribution of Hawk tail lengths have?

The point here is that number of modes is not a clearly defined concept. In this case there are two large modes which stands out with adjust=1 but the first mode appears to contain two smaller modes when plotted at a finer level of granularity with adjust=0.5. The number of modes is generally not a completely unambiguous concept.

6 Trend lines and facet wraps

Generate the following plot using the ggplot(), geom_point(), geom_smooth() and facet_wrap() functions.

g<-ggplot(data=hawksSmall,aes(x=Tail,y=Weight,color=Species))+
  geom_point()+xlab("Tail  (mm)")+ylab("Weight (gm)")+geom_smooth(method="lm")+
  theme_bw()+facet_wrap(~Species,scales = "free")

The visual cues include horizontal and vertical position, shape, color and direction.
